Pappardelle with crispy capers and red pepper

John Smith:

"An absolute winner of a dinner that will please all eaters alike. The balance of the sweetness of the red pepper, saltiness of the capers, and acidity of the lemon will keep you coming up for more."

Ingredients

Serves 3-4

  • 500g pappardelle
  • 400g block tofu
  • Five red peppers
  • Two medium white onions
  • Ten cloves garlic
  • Two chillis
  • Six tablespoons capers
  • One lemon
  • Sea salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Dried oregano
  • Ground turmeric
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Method

Preheat the oven to 200°C/180°C fan.

Cut up the red peppers, onions, garlic and chillis and add to a glass baking dish. Glug over some extra virgin olive oil, making sure that everything is coated. Season with salt and pepper. Let this roast for around 40 minutes until soft.

When the vegetables have been in the oven for around half an hour, salt some water, bring to a boil, and cook the pappardelle as per the instructions.

Drain the capers and fry in 1 tablespoon of oil until they're nice and crispy - this should take between 6 and 10 minutes.

Whilst the pasta is cooking and the capers are frying, prepare the tofu. Mash the tofu in a bowl with the juice of the lemon, 1 tablespoon of oregano, salt and pepper.

Once the vegetables are soft, tip them into a blender (I use a Nutribullet), along with some salt, pepper, and 1 tablespoon of turmeric. The turmeric is not really for flavour, but more for its health benefits and the fact that it gives the sauce a wonderful colour. Blend this mixture until it is completely smooth, adding some water if need be.

Once the pappardelle has cooked, drain it, leaving behind 1 tablespoon of pasta water. Add the capers and the sauce in with the pasta and mix.

Split the pasta between the dishes and serve with the tofu mix (as a topping) and a lemony rocket salad.
